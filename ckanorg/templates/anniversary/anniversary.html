{{ _("From a frustrated student to global infrastructure") }}

{{ _("Inspired by CPAN — the Perl package archive — he built CKAN: a catalog for data. Two decades later, it powers national open data portals on every continent, from data.gov to open.canada.ca to datos.gob.mx.") }}

{{ _("CKAN is now recognized as a") }} {{ _("Digital Public Good") }}, {{ _("helping achieve 9 of the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals.") }}

2006
{{ _("CKAN v0.1 — the first commit") }}{{ _("Milestone") }}▼
Rufus Pollock releases the first version of CKAN under the Open Knowledge Foundation. Inspired by CPAN, it was a simple catalog for open datasets, built to solve a personal frustration: public data was impossible to find.
CKAN was never meant to become a big open source project. It started because I needed something that didn't exist — a CPAN for data.— Rufus Pollock, Founder
2009
data.gov.uk launches on CKANAdoption▼
The UK Government launches its official open data portal powered by CKAN — one of the first major government portals in the world to go live. It sets a precedent for national open data infrastructure and sparks global interest.
2010
CKAN 1.0 — production readyRelease▼
The first stable release brings critical improvements to authorization, search, and resource previews — establishing CKAN as production-ready infrastructure for governments and institutions.
2011
CKAN 1.3 — harvesting & federationRelease▼
CKAN 1.3 introduces harvesting capabilities, letting portals aggregate and import datasets from external sources. A pivotal step toward federated open data ecosystems.
2013
CKAN 2.0 & DATA.GOV migrationMilestone▼
A major architectural overhaul brings scalability and a powerful plugin system. The US Government migrates Data.gov to CKAN, consolidating hundreds of thousands of federal datasets.
The plugin architecture was a bet that the community would build things we hadn't thought of. That bet paid off.— Ian Ward, Lead Developer
2015
Linked data, previews & visualizationsRelease▼
CKAN 2.3 and 2.4 bring support for linked data and in-browser data visualizations. Open data becomes genuinely accessible, not just technically available.
2018
1,000+ active portals worldwideMilestone▼
CKAN surpasses 1,000 active data portals worldwide, spanning every continent. Datashades index tracks some of them.
2021
Datopian & Link Digital become co-stewardsCommunity▼
A new stewardship model is established. Datopian and Link Digital join as co-stewards. The Open Knowledge Foundation remains as purpose trustee, ensuring CKAN's open ethos endures.
2023
Recognized as a Digital Public GoodMilestone▼
CKAN is officially recognized as a Digital Public Good by the DPGA, reinforcing its role in achieving 9 of the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals.
2026
20th Anniversary — the celebration yearMilestone▼
CKAN turns 20. The global community gathers to celebrate two decades of open source, open data, and open collaboration. You're part of this moment.
